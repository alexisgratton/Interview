Interview with Melissa Hunter

By Alexis Gratton

1. Why do you want to be a designer?

I want to be a designer because I like to think it's something that I can do well, despite my initial lack of enthusiasm.

2. What is difficult or frustrating for you within design and how do you work to push past that?

Finding my own creativity with open ended concepts is one of my biggest hurdles in creating designs. I try to keep my inspiration strict and use those elements to move forwards.

3. Where do you see yourself doing after you finish this program?

If anything, I see myself probably going into something like copywriting or bring an editor after the fact. Design was a second choice for me. Writing is my preferred passion.

4. What excites you about design?

Well that's quite the open-ended question, what I find most "exciting" about design as a concept would mostly be the absolute wild things that the bowl of tapioca pudding, we call a brain can accomplish with just a few simple elements.

5. What worries you about design?

What worries me would be in a similar fashion to what excites me. With the rise of "modern" and "contemporary" design, nothing really seems fun anymore. Everything has to be sleek, and minimal, and it's just boring.

6. What is a website?

A website is a translation of numerous code inputs to present information between users through the internet. Can be made interesting or equally as bland through use of design elements.
